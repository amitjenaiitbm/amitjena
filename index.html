Hi, I am

Amit Jena.

I am a Ph.D. student at IITB-Monash Research Academy, Mumbai, India.
I am associated with IDC School of Design, IIT Bombay, India and Faculty of IT, Monash University, Australia.

My research interest is designing and evaluating user interfaces and visualisations for the masses. I am interested in how people interpret visualisations, and how visualisations can be used for communicating complex information to non-experts.

I am advised by Prof. Venkatesh Rajamanickam, Prof. Tim Dwyer, Dr. Ulrich Engelke, and Dr. Cecile Paris.

Projects

Communicating uncertainty in public trasnport: Melbourne and Mumbai
We are exploring the possibility of communicating the associated uncertainty with any means of public transport to the commuters. This we believe may help commuters make an informed decision while using the public transport. For this pur- pose, we are designing a mobile app interface and plan to conduct user study in two thriving cities of the world: Melbourne and Mumbai. (2018-Present)
● CityVis workshop PDF & Slide ● GitHub ●

The tale of India’s 8495 railway stations
I designed an interactive visualisation to see the spread of Railway Stations across India. We were interested to know the coordinates of the stations, their official names, station codes, states, zones and divisions in which they are, category and date since which they are functioning. This is a work in progress as recently the station categories has been revised to include passenger ammenities leading to better passenger satisfaction. (2017-Present)
